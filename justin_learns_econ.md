

### GDP

Total market value of all final goods and services produced within a country's borders in a given period.

Can be calculated three different ways:
1. **Production** approach: adds up the value of everything produced (counting only the final value), to avoid double-counting.
2. **Expenditure** approach adds up the everything spent: consumption + investment + government spending + net imports
3. **Income** approach adds up everything earned: wages, profits, interest and rent paid out.

This means:

Youy basically add up all the ddomestically produced goods & services that a nation purchases (consumers, businessess, government), and then add the value of any exports sold, and subract the value of any imports.

Net exports = exports - imports

Government purchases is not the same as government expenditure, which can often include transfer of cash. This is counted as consumer expendentiture or business investment when it is spent downstream.
